\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We introduced \methodname, a controlled behavioral benchmark for assistant-initiated bedtime nudges in late-night LLM conversations. Across 108 real OpenAI model generations, bedtime nudges appeared in only 2 responses (1.85\%), both as soft sign-offs in fatigued \gptfourmini cases. We observed no hard disengagements, no task refusals, and no nudges in the dependent profile. A bounded \wildchat audit reinforced the same point: sleep phrase matches often do not indicate unilateral assistant pressure to stop.

The key takeaway is that, in this tested setting, ``go to sleep'' behavior is not a robust generic mechanism. It appears to be rare, soft, and conditioned on model and prompt context. Broader claims require larger samples, additional model families, persistent-memory settings, human labels, and mechanistic analysis in open-weight models.
