# Downloaded Papers

PDFs are stored directly in this directory. Chunk PDFs for the most relevant mechanistic and personalization papers are in `papers/pages/`.

1. **Refusal in Language Models Is Mediated by a Single Direction**
   - Authors: Andy Arditi, Oscar Obeso, Aaquib Syed, Daniel Paleka, Nina Panickssery, Wes Gurnee, Neel Nanda
   - Year: 2024
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.11717
   - File: `2406.11717_refusal_in_language_models_is_mediated_by_a_single_direction.pdf`
   - Why relevant: Direct evidence for a unified low-dimensional refusal mechanism.

2. **There Is More to Refusal in Large Language Models than a Single Direction**
   - Authors: Faaiz Joad, Majd Hawasly, Sabri Boughorbel, Nadir Durrani, Husrev Taha Sencar
   - Year: 2026
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.02132
   - File: `2602.02132_there_is_more_to_refusal_in_large_language_models_than_a_single_direct.pdf`
   - Why relevant: Counterpoint showing multiple refusal/non-compliance directions with a shared behavioral control knob.

3. **Over-Refusal and Representation Subspaces**
   - Authors: Utsav Maskey, Mark Dras, Usman Naseem
   - Year: 2026
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.27518
   - File: `2603.27518_over_refusal_and_representation_subspaces_a_mechanistic_analysis_of_ta.pdf`
   - Why relevant: Separates harmful refusal from task-conditioned over-refusal, important for bedtime nudges that may be safe-boundary behavior rather than harmful-content refusal.

4. **Refusal Direction is Universal Across Safety-Aligned Languages**
   - Authors: Xinpeng Wang, Mingyang Wang, Yihong Liu, Hinrich Schutze, Barbara Plank
   - Year: 2025
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.17306
   - File: `2505.17306_refusal_direction_is_universal_across_safety_aligned_languages.pdf`
   - Why relevant: Tests cross-lingual universality of refusal directions.

5. **Surgical, Cheap, and Flexible: Mitigating False Refusal in Language Models via Single Vector Ablation**
   - Authors: Xinpeng Wang, Chengzhi Hu, Paul Rottger, Barbara Plank
   - Year: 2024
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.03415
   - File: `2410.03415_surgical_cheap_and_flexible_mitigating_false_refusal_in_language_model.pdf`
   - Why relevant: Shows a separable false-refusal vector can reduce over-refusal while preserving safety.

6. **No for Some, Yes for Others: Persona Prompts and Other Sources of False Refusal in Language Models**
   - Authors: Flor Miriam Plaza-del-Arco, Paul Rottger, Nino Scherrer, Emanuele Borgonovo, Elmar Plischke, Dirk Hovy
   - Year: 2025
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.08075
   - File: `2509.08075_no_for_some_yes_for_others_persona_prompts_and_other_sources_of_false.pdf`
   - Why relevant: Directly evaluates whether sociodemographic personas change false refusal rates.

7. **XSTest: A Test Suite for Identifying Exaggerated Safety Behaviours in Large Language Models**
   - Authors: Paul Rottger, Hannah Rose Kirk, Bertie Vidgen, Giuseppe Attanasio, Federico Bianchi, Dirk Hovy
   - Year: 2023
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.01263
   - File: `2308.01263_xstest_a_test_suite_for_identifying_exaggerated_safety_behaviours_in_l.pdf`
   - Why relevant: Standard false-refusal benchmark with safe prompts that resemble unsafe prompts.

8. **OR-Bench: An Over-Refusal Benchmark for Large Language Models**
   - Authors: Justin Cui, Wei-Lin Chiang, Ion Stoica, Cho-Jui Hsieh
   - Year: 2024
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.20947
   - File: `2405.20947_or_bench_an_over_refusal_benchmark_for_large_language_models.pdf`
   - Why relevant: Large-scale over-refusal benchmark and safety/helpfulness trade-off analysis.

9. **SORRY-Bench: Systematically Evaluating Large Language Model Safety Refusal**
   - Authors: Tinghao Xie, Xiangyu Qi, Yi Zeng, Yangsibo Huang, Udari Madhushani Sehwag, Kaixuan Huang, Luxi He, Boyi Wei, et al.
   - Year: 2024
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.14598
   - File: `2406.14598_sorry_bench_systematically_evaluating_large_language_model_safety_refu.pdf`

10. **Do-Not-Answer: A Dataset for Evaluating Safeguards in LLMs**

    - File: `2308.13387_do_not_answer_a_dataset_for_evaluating_safeguards_in_llms.pdf`

11. **Persona Vectors: Monitoring and Controlling Character Traits in Language Models**
